## Introduction

ICDS V3 introduces several highly anticipated features such as dark mode, as well as… This is a comprehensive guide on how to upgrade your V2 app to V3. For a full list of the changes made in V3, please read the <ic-link href="/">3.x changelog</ic-link>.

### Theming

One of the biggest features in V3 is dark mode. With this new capability, you can set whether a component responds to changes in the system's light and dark theme or whether it always appears in a particular mode. As part of dark mode, we've adopted a design tokens approach in our components.

### Migration of multi-select functionality into ic-select

`ic-multi-select` (`IcMultiSelect`) is no longer a standalone component. The multi-select functionality can now be found in the `ic-select` (`IcSelect`) component.

```tsx
// v2
<ic-select-with-multi
  id="select-default"
  label="What are your favourite types of coffee?"
  multiple="true"
></ic-select-with-multi>

// v3
<ic-select
  id="select-default"
  label="What are your favourite types of coffee?"
  multiple="true"
></ic-select>
```

## Prop Renames

### Replace `appearance` with `theme` and `monochrome` props

Following the introduction of dark mode, we have replaced the `appearance` prop with the `theme` and `monochrome` props where applicable. The `theme` prop manages a component's light or dark variant.

### Sizing

We've made our sizing easier to understand by changing instances of `size=”default”` to `size=”medium and replacing the `small`prop with`size=”small”`. These changes may affect any existing tests you have.

#### Replace `size=”default”` with `“size=”medium”`

#### Replace `small={true}` with `“size=”small”`

### Rename `ic-data-entity` to `ic-data-list`

To clarify its use, we've renamed the `ic-data-entity` (`IcDataEntity`) component to `ic-data-list` (`IcDataList`).

### Rename `ic-card` to `ic-card-vertical`

With the introduction of the `ic-card-horizontal` (`IcCardHorizontal`) component, we have renamed `ic-card` (`IcCard`), to `ic-card-vertical` (`IcCardVertical`), to help differentiate between the two card components.
